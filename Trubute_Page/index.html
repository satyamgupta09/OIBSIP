MS Dhoni

Mahendra Singh Dhoni (born 7 July 1981) is a former Indian cricketer and captain of the Indian national cricket team. Dhoni is considered one of the greatest captains in the history of cricket.

Early life and cricket career

  • Dhoni was born in Ranchi, Bihar (now Jharkhand), and he comes from a middle-class family.
  • He excelled in multiple sports during his school days, including football and badminton.
  • Dhoni developed a passion for cricket and joined a local cricket club in his teenage years.
  • He made his first-class debut for Bihar (now Jharkhand) in 1999-2000.
  • Dhoni played his first ODI for India in December 2004 against Bangladesh.

Captaincy and Achievements

  • Dhoni became the captain of the Indian cricket team in 2007.
  • Under his captaincy, India won the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup in 2007.
  • Dhoni led India to victory in the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011, and he famously hit the winning six in the final against Sri Lanka.
  • He also led India to victory in the ICC Champions Trophy in 2013.
  • Dhoni is known for his cool and calm demeanor on the field, earning him the nickname "Captain Cool."
  • He holds several records as a captain, including the most Test wins and most wins in T20Is for India.

Rise to Stardom

  • Dhoni's explosive batting and his ability to finish matches earned him a reputation as one of the best finishers in the game.
  • He is known for his unorthodox 'helicopter shot,' which has become one of his trademark strokes.
  • Dhoni is one of the most successful Indian Premier League (IPL) captains, leading the Chennai Super Kings to multiple titles.
  • He was the recipient of the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award in 2007 and the Padma Shri in 2009.
  • Dhoni retired from international cricket in August 2020, leaving behind a legacy of success and leadership.

Personal life

  • Dhoni married Sakshi Singh Rawat in 2010, and they have a daughter named Ziva.
  • He is known for his humility and sportsmanship, earning the respect of players and fans worldwide.
